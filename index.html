About Derek S. Prijatelj

Hello there and welcome!

I am a researcher of information theory and machine learning, which means I study the theory and application of computation and statistics. I am a holistic researcher interested in understanding and modeling complex systems, especially artificial intelligent agents that will encounter uncertainty and novelty in their environment and must learn or adapt to maintain task performance.

I recieved my Ph.D. at the University of Notre Dame with Dr. Walter J. Scheirer as my advisor. My dissertation was on "Uncertainty and Novelty in Machine Learning" where I explored both the applications and the theory of how uncertainty and novelty may be encountered and learned by machines.

I started setting up my blog that will cover my research and related interests. Unsurprisingly, this has turned into a bit of a project. Fortunately, I'll have something others may be interested in once finished. For now, this website serves as hub of my linked internet presence.
